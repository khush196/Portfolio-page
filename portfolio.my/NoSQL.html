What is NoSQL?

NoSQL databases provide a mechanism for storing and retrieving data that is modeled differently than traditional relational databases. They are optimized for specific use cases such as handling large volumes of unstructured data, offering high scalability, and enabling faster operations.

Basic Concepts of NoSQL

Key-Value Stores

A key-value store is the simplest form of NoSQL database, where data is stored as a collection of key-value pairs, similar to a dictionary.

Document-Oriented Databases

Document databases store data in documents (like JSON or BSON) which provide flexibility and ease of access to complex structures.

Column-Family Databases

Data is stored in columns rather than rows, allowing efficient access and storage for large datasets, commonly used in data analytics.
